Preterm Childbirth Visualization

About

Preterm birth is defined as the delivery of a baby before 37 weeks of pregnancy. Current preterm birth rate in the U.S. is 9.6%, which is ranked grade C by March of Dimes premature birth report card. Preterm birth can lead to many health problems for infants, including brain damage, neurological disorders, asthma, intestinal problems and vision problems. The exact cause of preterm birth is unclear, but several risk factors including smoking, alcohol, illegal drug usage, stress, poor nutrition and poor health of the mother, and family violence are confirmed. In this visualization, you will view the prevalence of preterm birth in the U.S. During 2010 and 2014 based on Metropolitan Statistical Areas (MSAs).
